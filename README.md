# APT29 (Cozy Bear) Intelligence Risk Assessment

This project is a 19-page strategic intelligence risk assessment of the Russian state-sponsored cyber threat group APT29 (Cozy Bear), written from the perspective of the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA).

The assessment evaluates the risk posed by long-term cyber espionage campaigns targeting U.S. federal civilian executive branch (FCEB) networks, with a focus on large-scale data theft and its strategic consequences.

## Key Focus Areas
- Threat actor capabilities and strategic intent
- Risks to federal civilian agencies
- Intelligence-driven risk modeling
- Structured analytic techniques (KAC, Indicators & Signposts)
- Mitigation and consequence management recommendations

## Methods & Frameworks Used
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT)
- Intelligence risk assessment methodologies
- Structured analytical techniques
- Policy and strategic analysis
